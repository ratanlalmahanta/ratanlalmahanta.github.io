1. Merton Structural Model

The Merton model links a firm's equity and debt structure to the probability of default. Under this framework, the firm defaults if the asset value falls below the debt at maturity.

$$ P_D = \mathbb{P}(V_T < D) = \Phi\left(-d_2\right) $$

where \( V_T \) is the asset value at maturity, \( D \) is the debt obligation, and \( \Phi \) is the standard normal CDF.

2. Estimating Asset Volatility

Equity volatility \( \sigma_E \) is observable, but asset volatility \( \sigma_V \) must be inferred using It√¥'s Lemma:

$$ \sigma_E = \frac{V}{E} \cdot \frac{\partial E}{\partial V} \cdot \sigma_V $$

This relationship helps back out \( \sigma_V \) using market equity data and a numerical solver.

3. CDS Pricing from Default Probability

Once we have the risk-neutral probability of default \( P_D \), the CDS spread can be approximated as:

$$ \text{CDS Spread} \approx \frac{(1 - \text{Recovery Rate}) \cdot P_D}{\text{Duration}} $$

This assumes constant hazard rate and flat yield curves.
